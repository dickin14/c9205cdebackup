Here we'll go through the types of classes available to be played in FFXIV, and the ones you can start off with.
The class you pick determines which city you start in. In addition, once you reach a certain point, you can unlock the other classes! Click on a class to see which city they belong to.

Gladiator

Gladiator, the sword and shield tank. They use their high defense to take the brunt of enemy attacks.

Marauder

Marauder, the axe tank. They use their high health and brute strength to occupy the enemy.

Conjurer

Conjurer, the spiritual healer. They use their cane and the power of the elementals around them to heal their comrades.

Pugilist

Pugilist, the fist-fighting damage dealer. They use their speed and power to deliver flurries of blows.

Lancer

Lancer, the spear-wielding damage dealer. They use their weapon to deliver precise, powerful strikes.

Rogue

Rogue, the stealthy damage dealer. They use their daggers to deliver quick, debilitating attacks.

Archer

Archer, the long-range fighter. They use their bow to deliver rapid shots from afar.

Thaumaturge

Thaumaturge, the elementalist. They use the power of fire and ice to deliver powerful blasts and crippling strikes.

Arcanist

Arcanist, the scholastic caster. They use their pets and spells to deliver enervating ailments.

A tank class is one that focuses on keeping the enemies attention by generating enmity (frequently called 'aggro', short for aggravation, or 'hate'
They do this so that their allies can focus on dispatching the enemies without having to worry about getting attacked themselves.
As such, tanks aren't the party members who deal the highest damage, but they are vital to success, and are often the leaders of their groups.

A healer class is one that focuses on making sure their party members stay alive by healing their allies and removing any ailments or 'debuffs'.
They can also give slight boosts to their party in the form of 'buffs', statuses which arae helpful to the people they are affecting.
As such, healers are the heart of the party, making sure everyone can do their job by keeping them healthy, and also dealing some damage too if they have time.

A DPS (Damage Per Second) class is on that focuses on subduing enemies by dealing as much damage (per second) as they can (sometimes also called Damage Dealers).
They all have their own positive statuses (buffs) that they can give to themselves or their party to help deal damage, or survive oncoming attacks.
As such, the DPS are the bread and butter of the party. Since there are a lot of these classes, there's lots of variety to pick from, but also a lot of competition!
